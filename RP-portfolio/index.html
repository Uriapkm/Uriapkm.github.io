About Me

The bridge between scientific ideas and functional prototypes

Functional Prototyping Engineer for Battery Research

The challenge: Battery research is complex, expensive, and slow. Researchers waste valuable time and resources on generic equipment that doesn't quite fit their experiments.

My approach: I'm the bridge between scientific concepts and working prototypes. By combining precision engineering, additive manufacturing, and automation, I solve real lab problems. From parametric adapters that replace €8,000+ equipment to automated systems with 25 Python-controlled pumps.

The outcome: Researchers save thousands of euros, get more reliable data, and speed up their experiments. My approach integrates CAD design (FreeCAD/OpenSCAD), programming (Python/Arduino), and hands-on fabrication to deliver turnkey solutions.

With over 6 years at CIC energiGUNE, I've developed 50+ functional prototypes specializing in test benches for redox flow batteries, electroplating systems, and electrochemical characterization.

How I Work

My process: design → fabrication → testing → iteration

1. Discovery

I understand the researcher's real problem: What do they need to measure? What are the limitations of current equipment? What's the budget and timeline?

2. Design

Parametric CAD modeling in FreeCAD/OpenSCAD, basic simulations if needed, and iterative review with the end user.

3. Fabrication

3D printing (FDM/resin), CNC machining when precision is required, electronic component assembly, and embedded programming.

4. Validation

Testing under real laboratory conditions, fine-tuning, complete documentation, and training for the research team.
